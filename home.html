FAQ

What is IGCSE?

IGCSE stands for International General Certificate of Secondary Education. It is an internationally recognized qualification for students, usually taken at the end of the secondary school phase.

How many subjects do students typically take in IGCSE?

Students usually take between 8 to 10 subjects in IGCSE. This includes core subjects like English, Mathematics, and Science, along with a selection of additional subjects based on their interests and academic goals.

How are IGCSE exams structured?

IGCSE exams are typically divided into core and extended papers. The core papers cover essential topics, while the extended papers include more challenging questions. The grading scale ranges from A* to G, with A* being the highest grade.

What is the difference between IGCSE and other educational systems?

IGCSE is known for its international approach to education, focusing on a broad and balanced curriculum. It is recognized by universities and employers worldwide. Unlike some national systems, IGCSE is not limited to a specific country's educational framework, making it adaptable for students globally.
